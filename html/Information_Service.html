What does
Information Service
do?

Information Systems Security (InfoSec) protects information systems, and the data that resides on them, from unauthorized access and disruption of access to authorized users. The triad of InfoSec contains three fundamental components: Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA). Applying solid and proven InfoSec protections with continuous review for effectiveness is the first defensive measure considered by SECCON. SECCON's Information Assurance team uses tools with the ability to detect anomalous activities outside a known state, analyze (may be nothing or a big something) the activity and then, if necessary, respond to the activity which may include shutting a communication pathway off for a period of time or to effect removal of an infected system (quarantine) etc. SECCON has developed a continuous circle of protect, detect, respond that has proven effective to our clients.

The Nigeria private security industry contributes an estimated 0.7% to GDP, with over N550b in supply chain value. In two decades of close watch, we have observed the industry appreciating out of mediocrity to a structured profitable service business, and a performing corporate business unit. Hence, adding value to the overall organisational growth and the national economy. The drivers of change include the rising global and regional insecurity and the awareness of potential risk by the public, the increasing demand for highly skilled risk management professionals with the relevant education and training, and intense competition by the growing number of service providers. In addition, technology advancement and process improvement from the old traditional security methods which relied on isolation (security-by-obscurity) as the preferred risk mitigation strategy, to a well-defined, well-designed, and well-implemented integrated physical protection security system. Thus, enabling the value of the Internet of Things (IoT). Not only is this convergence in security helping information technology and operational technology managers to improve productivity, but also, increasing operating equipment effectiveness.
